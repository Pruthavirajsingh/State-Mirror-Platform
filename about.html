TIES Research

Built to map power, money, and influence.

This project is a living research interface for exploring political networks, public disclosures, and connected entities in a clear, visual way. It is designed to help users navigate complex relationships quickly while keeping the presentation simple, readable, and grounded.

Trace connections
Follow links between leaders, parties, companies, donors, and legal controversies in a single map.
Compare networks
Switch between parties and instantly compare funding, assets, symbols, and key relationships.
Inspect details
Select a node to see contextual notes, linked entities, and source-backed summaries.
Stay oriented
Use chips, search, and color cues to move through dense political data without losing context.
Work in progress
The product is actively evolving, so data, labels, and mappings may improve as the research set expands.
Use Cases

Investigative journalism

Use the graph to quickly uncover party-linked companies, assets, donors, and legal pressure points.

Research briefing

Turn a dense set of affidavits and reports into a visual summary for fast internal review.

Election analysis

Compare political organizations by funding, leadership wealth, and controversy profile.

Classroom explanation

Show how political ecosystems connect across parties, regions, business interests, and institutions.

Editorial planning

Identify the most important nodes and the most visible relationships before publishing a story.

Pattern discovery

Spot repeated ties, recurring entities, and high-impact legal or financial intersections across parties.
